    • This is a website containing my personal opinons on three books I recently read. Book 1 is The Sun and The Star by Rick Riordan, book 2 is The Spirit Bares its Teeth by Andrew Joseph White, and finally book 3 is The Witch and the Vampire by Francesca Flores. I will be giving a summary of these books along with my personal opinion and my rating out of five.

    Book 1

    The Sun and The Star

    Summary

    The Sun and The Star is a book by Rick Riordan that follows the story of two demigods, Nico di Angelo and Will Solace, two characters in Rick Riordans ongoing Percy Jackson series. This book follows the characters as they go on a quest to save their friend, the book is extremely humourous, filled with adventure and emotion. Will Solace and Nico Di Angelo are two of the growing number of queer characters within this franchise and in order to properly write them the author has brought on queer co-author Mark Oshiro.

    My Opinion

    I really loved this book. I love Rick Riordans' choice to partner with a queer author so he could authentically write these characters, that is something any straight person writing queer characters should do, get insight from a queer person. I am so happy this book was released because these are two of my favourite characters and I love that they've been getting more attention recently. I thought this book was well written and very interesting, I love seeing queer people represented in media and there is actually a sequel to this book coming out later this year and I'm very excited to see what happens next.

    My Rating:

    Book 2

    The Spirit Bares its Teeth

    Summary

    The Spirit Bares its Teeth is a book by Andrew Joseph White that tells the story of Silas Bell a transgender boy in 1883 London. This book is set in a world where violet eyed mediums can commune with the spirits behind the thinned veil between life and death. After a failed attempt to escape an arranged marriage he is diagnosed with "veil sickness" a mysterious disease sending violet-eyed women into madness. He's shipped off to a "school" to cure him so he may be a proper future wife to his betrothed. The school is a cold mysterious facility where students either emerge perfect wives or they disappear, once more girls begin going missing Silas decides to find out the truth.

    My Opinion

    This book was incredible, it was slightly different to what I usually read but I was completely hooked. Silas is a surgeon so there are some rather graphic descriptions but I ended up learning some new terms. This book is so incredible in representing the transgender experience, it also deals with the toleration of autism and society's manipulation of women incredibly well. Andrew Joseph White has an incredible style of writing and I plan to read many more of his books.

    My Rating:

    Book 3

    The Witch and The Vampire

    Summary

    The Witch and The Vampire by Francesca Flores is a fantasy novel that follows the romance between Kaye (a flame witch) and Ava (a vampire). The two used to be friends, until one day the vampires broke through the protective magical barrier that protects their town, Kaye's mother is killed and Ava is turned into a vampire. The story starts two years later with Ava trapped in her house. Her mother Eugenia needs her: Ava still has her witch powers, and Eugenia must take them in order to hide that she's a vampire as well. Desperate to escape her confinement and stop her mother's plans to destroy the town, Ava must break out, flee to the forest, and seek help from the vampires who live there. When there is another attack, she sees her opportunity and escapes. Kaye is now at the end of her flame witch training, desperate to get out into the world and help protect the town from vampires. Ava and Kaye meet again in the forest and find out that Ava's mother has bigger plans than once thought, they must find a way to work together to save everyone.

    My Opinion

    This book was a great read. There was so much backstory and the world was slightly complex which is admittedly slightly confusing at times, but it's also weirdly easy and soothing. This book is wonderful if you're looking for a nice read it's mostly built on the girls individual inner turmoils while also having the romance between the two as the other main focus. I really enjoyed seeing lots of development within the characters throughout the book it has wonderful descriptions and is generally very pleseant. It unfortunately didn't score as highly as the other books just because it gtakes a little to really get into it and sometimes the backstory is slightly confusing, but I still recommend it if you want a fantasy queer romance.

    My Rating:
